DETROIT

I AM A BORN AND BRED DETROIT NATIVE.

My grand parents migrated here from Alabama in the 50s. They met at the infamous Train Station in southwest Detroit and began raising their family in the university district alongside some of their siblings who also moved here for work. From there they moved to other historic neighborhoods including the Boston Edison district, where my mother is currently a proud homeowner.

Wanting more than to leave one single home to their children, my grandparents moved deeper into Detroit's west side where the purchased a total of about six homes on on block and provide them to their siblings and children to live in. That soon became the norm as many of their neighbors began to do the same. At one point that particular block was chalked full of families who owned at least two homes within that one block. As a result, our neighbors became more like family. Many of my relatives still reside there today.

Those ties my grandparents built for my family serve as the root of my love for the city. I attended THE Cass Tech, home to countless famous and formidable technicians who are world changers today. My very first job was with a small black-owned law firm housed in the Ford building downtown. I earned my Degrees from Wayne State University and purchased my first home in the historic LaSalle Gardens neighborhood.

Detroit is home to a collection of people full of grit, tenacity, pride and talent. It birthed greatness including Motown Records, some of the nation's first vehicles and the country's first mile of concrete highway. The city turned a bankrupt circus into a zoo--the first to use bar-less exhibits extensively by the way! Ultimately it's people like those represented within the citylimits of Detroit who push the boundaries of our imagination and produce greatness from the absence of basic necessities. I take that spirit with me on every endeavor of my life.
